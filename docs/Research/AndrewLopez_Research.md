# Status Tracking App

A digital tool designed to monitor, log, and visualize the progress of various items, tasks, habits, or conditions over time. Essentially, it answers the question: *"Where does this specific thing stand right now, and how has it changed?"* 

Because "status" can mean many things, these apps span several different categories depending on what you are trying to track.

---

## Core & Essential Features

### 1. Project & Task Management
In a professional or team setting, these apps track the lifecycle of work items. They often use Kanban boards (columns like *To Do*, *In Progress*, *Under Review*, *Done*) to show where a project stands.

* **Examples:** Trello, Asana, Monday.com, Jira
* **Best For:** Knowing who is doing what and when a project will be finished.

### 2. Habit & Goal Trackers
For personal development, these apps track whether you are maintaining daily routines or hitting milestones. They often use streaks, calendars, and charts to keep you motivated.

* **Examples:** Streaks, Habitica, Way of Life
* **Best For:** Tracking daily habits (e.g., "Drank 8 glasses of water," "Exercised").

### 3. Health & Symptom Trackers
These are specialized apps used to monitor physical or mental well-being over time. Users log daily data to find patterns or share updates with doctors.

* **Examples:** Clue (cycle tracking), Bearable (symptom/mood tracking), MyFitnessPal (weight/calorie tracking)
* **Best For:** Monitoring chronic illness, fitness goals, or mental health.

### 4. Logistics & Order Trackers
Apps that aggregate delivery data from various courier services to tell you exactly where a package is in the shipping process.

* **Examples:** Shop, Route, AfterShip
* **Best For:** Seeing when your online shopping orders will arrive at your door.

## Platform Research

The following platforms closely resemble the core features we want to explore. Studying them highlights why specific features are crucial to building an effective tracking tool.

| Platform | Why Look at It? | Key Value / Inspiration |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Jira** | Excellent reference for complex status logic. Teaches how to map strict permissions (e.g., *Only a Manager can move a task from "Review" to "Approved"*) and deep issue tracking. | Highly relevant to the **GitHub Actions** features we want to implement into our personal projects. |
| **Linear** | A newer, incredibly fast alternative to Jira that product and engineering teams love. | The absolute best reference for **speed, keyboard shortcuts, and minimalist UI**. It proves a status tracker can feel lightweight despite holding massive amounts of technical data. |
